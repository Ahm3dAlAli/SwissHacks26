ReduitTreasuries MVP Product Design DocumentProduct summaryReduitTreasuries is a treasury-as-a-service prototype for multinational groups that need tomove liquidity between subsidiaries faster, reduce idle balances, and optionally extend shortterm internal working-capital credit. The MVP is designed for an XRPL hackathon and focuseson one realistic institutional workflow: HQ treasury funds a subsidiary instantly over XRPLsettlement rails, then optionally issues a 7-day internal liquidity line backed by pooled treasuryliquidity.[cite:165][cite:32][cite:112]Version 2 extends the product with an agent layer. The agent layer does not replace treasuryapproval; it continuously forecasts near-term cash needs, identifies sweepable surplus, detectsearly working-capital stress in subsidiaries or supply-chain counterparties, and recommendsor executes permitted actions under explicit policy controls.[cite:176][cite:179][cite:181][cite:190][cite:196]The product remains intentionally narrow so it can be built and tested during a hackathon. Itcombines the challenge's strongest tracks—cross-border payments and FX, credit and lending,and agentic finance—without overcommitting to production-scale banking integrations.[cite:165][cite:9][cite:32][cite:176]Problem statementMultinational treasury teams o en face four recurring pain points:Cross-border intercompany funding is slow because settlement depends on banking cut-offwindows and fragmented rails.Cash is trapped in different subsidiaries instead of being concentrated or reused efficiently.A subsidiary with a temporary working-capital gap o en has to wait for manual approvalsor external bank lines even when another entity in the group has excess liquidity.Treasury teams usually react too late to emerging supply-chain credit stress becausewarning signals appear first in cash timing, payment delays, and working-capital patternsrather than in formal defaults.[cite:187][cite:189][cite:191][cite:194]The hackathon prototype addresses these pain points by using XRPL-native or XRPL-alignedprimitives: fast settlement rails, permissioned participation controls, pooled liquidity viaSingle Asset Vaults, fixed-term lending logic via XLS-66, and a governed agent layer thatimproves timing and quality of treasury actions.[cite:112][cite:165][cite:32][cite:176]Product conceptReduitTreasuries acts as a corporate treasury operating layer on XRPL. It lets a parent treasurysettle internal cross-border flows quickly, sweep excess balances into a pooled liquidity vault,extend short-term internal credit lines, and use an agent system to forecast liquidity andproactively detect future credit needs across subsidiaries and supply-chain participants.[cite:165][cite:32][cite:176][cite:190]The product is not positioned as retail DeFi. It is internal or permissioned treasuryinfrastructure for controlled counterparties, with policy-driven approvals, audit trails, andclearly scoped autonomous actions.[cite:112][cite:133][cite:176]Users and rolesPrimary usersGroup Treasurer: owns liquidity policy, approves intercompany funding, monitorsbalances.Subsidiary Finance Manager: requests funding, receives settlement, repays short-term line.Treasury Operator: executes approved transfers and monitors statuses.Risk/Compliance Reviewer: verifies eligible entities and permitted inbound flows.Supply Chain Finance Analyst: monitors supplier or distributor stress signals and reviewsagent recommendations.System rolesParent Treasury Wallet: master settlement and liquidity source wallet.Subsidiary Wallets: dedicated wallets for each entity.Liquidity Vault: pooled liquidity source used for temporary credit lines.[cite:172][cite:165]Policy Engine: off-chain rules service deciding whether a transfer, sweep, or credit line isallowed.Treasury Agent: forecasting, sweep recommendation, routing, and early-warningorchestration layer.[cite:176][cite:181][cite:196]Core MVP use caseThe MVP proves two connected end-to-end scenarios:

Switzerland HQ has excess RLUSD-equivalent settlement balance.

Brazil subsidiary needs urgent liquidity.

HQ treasury approves an intercompany transfer.

Funds settle on XRPL in seconds.

If the required amount exceeds the instant transfer allocation, the subsidiary draws a 7-dayinternal working-capital line sourced from a vault-backed liquidity pool.

The dashboard records approval, settlement, drawdown, maturity date, and repaymentstatus.[cite:165][cite:32][cite:112]Second scenario:

The agent detects that a subsidiary or key supply-chain participant is likely to face a shortterm cash gap in the next few days.

The agent flags the risk, recommends pausing a sweep or pre-positioning funds, andproposes either an internal transfer or a short-term liquidity line.

Treasury approves the proposed action.

The action is executed and logged with rationale, policy match, and expected impact.[cite:179][cite:181][cite:190][cite:196]ScopeIn scopeTreasury dashboard for HQ and subsidiary views.Wallet-based Entity and wallet managementThe system must support one HQ entity and at least two subsidiaries.Each entity must have a linked XRPL wallet address.The UI must display wallet role, jurisdiction, and available balance.

Funding request workflowA subsidiary must be able to create a funding request with amount, currency rail, reason,and due date.HQ must be able to approve or reject the request.Approved requests must generate a settlement instruction.

Transfer settlementThe system must submit or simulate an XRPL payment from HQ wallet to subsidiarywallet.The UI must show statuses: Dra , Pending Approval, Approved, Submitted, Settled, Failed.Transfer records must include transaction hash or simulated reference.

Permission controlsThe system must maintain an allowlist of approved entities.The system must prevent transfer execution to non-approved subsidiary wallets.If DepositAuth / DepositPreauth configuration is implemented, the UI must reflect whetherinbound payments are allowed.[cite:112]

Liquidity vault moduleHQ must be able to deposit excess liquidity into a pooled treasury vault.The UI must show vault balance, contributed amount, and available-for-lending amount.The system may simulate vault shares if direct XLS-65 integration is not available in thedemo environment, but the UX must match the real concept of pooled single-assetdeposits.[cite:172][cite:165]

Short-term credit lineHQ must be able to approve a short-term internal line for a subsidiary.Credit line must include principal, term, maturity date, and status.The system must record whether the line is funded from available vault liquidity.The system must support repayment and status update to Closed.[cite:32]

Dashboard metricsThe dashboard must show:Pending vs settled transfers.Idle cash moved to vault.Credit line outstanding.Estimated treasury efficiency gains, using mock comparisons against bank-delaybaseline.Agent watchlist of predicted shortfalls and sweepable surpluses.

Audit trailAll actions must be logged: request created, approval, transfer submitted, transfer settled,vault deposit, credit line created, repayment.Agent-generated recommendations and policy decisions must also be logged.Logs must be filterable by entity and action type.

Agent forecasting and sweep logicThe system must calculate short-horizon projected cash position for each entity.The system must estimate sweepable surplus using a minimum operating buffer.The system must recommend a sweep when projected balances remain above thresholda er expected outflows.[cite:179][cite:181][cite:187]The system must block or warn against a sweep if forecasted balance falls below threshold.[cite:187][cite:192]

Supply chain credit radarThe system must produce an early-warning score for future credit need.The score must use explainable rule-based inputs such as falling projected cash balance,rising payment delay, inventory stress, or mismatch between financing usage and activity.[cite:189][cite:191][cite:195][cite:198]When the score exceeds threshold, the agent must recommend one of the followingactions:pause sweep,send treasury transfer,create short-term line,escalate for manual review.Non-functional requirementsWeb-based interface usable during a live demo.Clear UX for non-crypto-native treasury users.No raw blockchain jargon on primary screens unless needed.Reliable fallback mode if testnet/devnet service is unstable.Explainable agent reasoning visible in UI.Data persisted locally in application memory or mock backend for the demo.Agent strategyThe agent strategy is to use a governed, explainable, low-risk automation model rather than ablack-box autonomous finance agent. The treasury agent operates in three levels:

Observe: ingest balances, requests, projected inflows/outflows, recent payment behavior,and selected supply-chain indicators.[cite:181][cite:187][cite:199]

Recommend: produce a forecast, compute surplus and deficit projections, and suggestsweep, transfer, or credit actions.[cite:179][cite:181][cite:196]

Execute under policy: automatically execute only narrowly scoped actions, such as a sweepbelow a threshold to an approved wallet, while larger or riskier actions require humanapproval.[cite:176][cite:112]This strategy makes the product more credible for institutional judges because the agentimproves timing and efficiency without pretending to replace treasury governance.[cite:176][cite:178]Agent design architectureAgent modulesModuleCash ForecastAgentSweepOptimizer AgentPurposePredict near-termliquidity by entityDetect excess cash andrecommend sweepsInputsbalances, scheduled payables, expectedinflows, historical outflowsOutputsprojected balance curve,deficit/surplus alertprojected balances, minimum buffers,policy limitsFunding RouterAgentSupply ChainCredit RadarChoose best treasuryresponseDetect likely need forshort-term creditdeficit amount, source balances, vaultcapacity, policy constraintssweep recommendation orno-actionrecommend transfer vsinternal linepayment delays, order changes,inventory movement, projected cash gapPolicy GuardAudit CopilotEnforce allowedautonomous behaviorExplain why the actionwas takenapproval matrix, corridor rules, amountlimits, entity permissionsearly-warning score, creditalertallow, block, requireapprovalrecommendation inputs, policy match,user actionAgent data flowrationale text and audit entry
